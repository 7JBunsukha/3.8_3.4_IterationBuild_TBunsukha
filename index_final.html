Classical Antiquity

Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome



Classical antiquity is a period of cultural history consisting of the ancient Greek and Roman society and culture, this period is also referred to as the Graeco-Roman world. This is the period where the ancient Greek and Roman society prospered and had influence over much of the Mediterranean sea. The classical antiquity period began in the 8th century BCE with the earliest recorded Greek epic from the Greek poetic Homer to and ended in 5th century CE with the fall of the western Roman empire.

Ancient Rome

Ancient Rome began in the 8th century BCE starting with the founding of the city of Rome and ended with the fall of the western Roman empire in the 5th century CE. The ancient Roman period encompassed the Roman kingdom, the Roman republic. The ancient Romans created one of the largest empires in the ancient world with the population of Rome reaching one million and left behind many things from their society such as writings and architecture. According to Roman legend, the city was founded by Romulus after he killed his brother Remus when arguing where to build their city.
